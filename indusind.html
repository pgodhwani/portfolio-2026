About the Project

The Challenge

A Bit Dull, Honestly

IndusInd Bank carries a strong premium identity — but their existing credit card app experience didn't match that promise. The design system felt flat, generic, and dated compared to the brand's aspirational positioning.

The brief was clear: establish a premium digital experience across IndusInd's credit card portfolio — one that felt worthy of the card itself.

1

Modernise the credit card upgrade UX — reduce drop-off and increase card activations

2

Create a visual design language that communicates premium without being intimidating

3

Build a scalable design system adaptable across campaigns, seasons, and card variants

The Product

A Unified Credit Card App

The team set out to build a consumer-facing application that lets users view and manage their IndusInd credit cards from a single, elegant interface. The app needed to handle a broad set of everyday card tasks without feeling complex or transactional.

Their end-to-end experience spans four key flows:

01Card Activation
02Spend Tracking
03EMI & Limits
04Offers & Rewards

Each capability was designed to feel seamless and premium — not like a banking utility, but like a personal financial companion.

  • View and manage spend across multiple IndusInd credit cards
  • Track transactions and set custom spend limits
  • Convert transactions to EMI with a few taps
  • Access curated offers and card benefits at a glance

Process

Moodboarding to Align Stakeholders

Before any UI was designed, the team used moodboarding as a strategic tool — to align stakeholder perspectives, pressure-test aesthetic directions, and explore the full range of what "premium" could mean for IndusInd's diverse card user base.

1

Gather visual references across fintech, luxury, and Indian consumer brands

2

Organise design elements, typography pairings, and colour palettes per direction

3

Present aesthetic directions to stakeholders and gather structured feedback

4

Analyse tone and resonance of each direction against IndusInd's brand values

5

Map audience segments to directions — premium vs. mass-affluent vs. aspirational

6

Converge on a final direction and define the design language for the product

Design Directions

Three Paths to Premium

Three distinct aesthetic directions were developed and explored to capture different facets of what premium could mean for IndusInd's credit card experience.

Dark & Modern

Dark & Modern

Deep navy and obsidian backgrounds with gold accents — evoking exclusivity and sophistication. Inspired by luxury fintech apps and Black-tier card aesthetics.

Soft & Bubblegum

Soft & Bubblegum

Pastel pinks, lavenders, and playful gradients — approachable and aspirational. Targeting younger, first-time premium card holders discovering lifestyle benefits.

Indic & Vibrant

Indic & Vibrant

Saffron, marigold, and jewel tones rooted in Indian visual culture — bold, celebratory, and deeply resonant with India's festive and aspirational consumer spirit.

Each direction was accompanied by typography explorations, iconography styles, card skin concepts, and micro-animation references — giving stakeholders a complete picture before committing to a path.
